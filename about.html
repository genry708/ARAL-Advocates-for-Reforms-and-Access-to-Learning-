How ARAL Came to Be

ARAL (Advocates for Reforms and Access to Learning) began as a personal vision of mine. As a student, I have always been aware of the challenges that many children face in accessing quality education, especially those in rural and marginalized areas. At first, it was just me ‚Äî one learner with the dream of helping others learn, despite the barriers of poverty, distance, and lack of resources. I started by researching the realities of education inequality and sharing them with my classmates and teachers.

Over time, others who shared the same passion joined me. From being a solo effort, ARAL has grown into a small but dedicated group of young advocates working together to spread awareness, push for reforms, and support learners in need. What started with one voice has become a collective movement that believes in one mission: to make sure no learner is left behind.
